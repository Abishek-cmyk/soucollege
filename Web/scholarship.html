Students' Aid Fund

  • Each student is required to pay ₹3 every academic year at the time of admission. This contribution is voluntary and non-refundable. The collected amount, along with government grants, is deposited in a separate bank account operated by the Principal.
  • The objective of the fund is to provide financial assistance to poor and deserving students for tuition fees, examination fees, books, and other essential expenses. Limited assistance may also be offered for hostel mess, clothing, or medical needs if considered genuine. Interest-free loans up to 50% may also be granted. No scholarship or stipend is given from this fund.
  • Assistance may be cancelled or forfeited for misconduct, irregular attendance, or failure to make satisfactory progress. The fund cannot be used for giving prizes or rewards. Any unspent balance may be carried forward to the following year.
  • The Students Aid Fund Committee determines the quantum of assistance. The committee consists of the Principal as Ex-officio Chairman, one teaching faculty member, and one student member, nominated by the Principal.
  • The Principal is empowered to frame, alter, or supplement rules as required by the University Grants Commission. Contributions by the Management Council are not permissible, though individual committee members may contribute personally if they wish.
  • Accounts of the Students’ Aid Fund shall be audited by a qualified Chartered Accountant, and audit fees or related expenditure will not be met from the fund.
